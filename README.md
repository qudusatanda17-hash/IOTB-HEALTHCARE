# ANALYSIS OF IOTB TECH HOSPITAL RECORDS

This report summarizes the healthcare dataset provided for IOTB TECH Hospital. The file contains 55,500 unique patient records and 16 fields, covering patient demographics, admission patterns, medical conditions, billing, medication usage, and test results.

## 1. Key Findings

| Analysis Area | Result |
|-----------------------|----------|
| Total unique patients | 55,500 |
| Most common gender | Male (27,774) |
| Most common blood type | A- (6,969) |
| Largest age admitted | 38 (897) |
| Most common condition | Arthritis (9,308) |
| Condition with highest billing | Diabetes ($238,555,810) |
| Condition with longest stay | Asthma (15.7 days) |
| Highest-billing insurance provider | Cigna ($287,164,069) |
| Most common admission type | Elective (18,655) |
| Admission type with highest revenue | Elective ($477,654,210) |
| Most admission month | August (4,832 admissions) |
| Highest revenue month | July ($122,758,880) |
| Most prescribed medication | Lipitor (11,140) |
| Medication with highest billing | Ibuprofen ($286,383,991) |


## 2. Patient Analysis

- Gender distribution is almost perfectly balanced, with 27,774 male patients and 27,726 female patients.
- The most common blood group is A-, although all blood groups appear fairly evenly distributed.
- Patients at age 38 had the highest admissions at 897 patients.


## 3. Medical Condition Analysis

- Arthritis is the most common condition with 9,308 cases.
- Diabetes generates the highest total billing amount at $238,555,810
- Asthma has the longest average hospital stay at 15.7 days.


## 4. Revenue and Billing Analysis

- Total hospital billing is $1,417,539,892
- Cigna contributes the highest billing value among insurers at $287,164,069
- Elective admissions generate the highest revenue at $477,654,210


## 5. Admission Analysis

- Elective admissions are also the most common admission type with 18,655 records.
- August records the highest monthly admissions with 4,832 patients.
- Female patients outnumber male patients in emergency admissions, 9,244 versus 9,025.


## 6. Test Result Analysis

- There are 18,627 abnormal test results in the dataset.
- Among abnormal cases, arthritis appears most frequently with 3,188 records.


## 7. Medication Analysis

- Lipitor is the most prescribed medication with 11,140 prescriptions.
- Ibuprofen is associated with the highest cumulative billing amount at $286,383,991


## 8. Insights

- August was the busiest month for admissions, while July produced the highest monthly revenue, showing that even though the volume for the month of July is low but still the month with the highest revenue i.e. more higher volume does not mean higher revenue or Volume ≠ Revenue.
- Asthma has the longest average stay, which may reflect more intensive monitoring or slower recovery times.
- Patient demand is relatively balanced by gender, so the hospital should maintain service capacity for both male and female patients equally.
- Arthritis, diabetes, hypertension, obesity, asthma, and cancer are all represented at similar volumes, showing that the hospital treats a broad mix of chronic and long-term conditions.
- Diabetes creates the highest billing volume even though it is not the most common condition, suggesting that it is relatively costly to manage.
- Cigna is the largest insurer contributor by billing value, making it a strategically important payer relationship.
- Elective admissions lead both in count and revenue, which is pointed as a major revenue driver.
- Emergency admissions are slightly more common among female patients, which may be worth monitoring.
- Lipitor is the most prescribed medication, but ibuprofen generates the highest billing, indicating that prescription frequency and revenue contribution are not identical.


## 9. Recommendations

- Review diabetes-related care pathways and billing controls, since diabetes contributes the highest billing amount.
- Monitor long-stay conditions such as asthma to improve bed turnover and discharge planning.
- Maintain close coordination with top insurers, especially Cigna, because insurer mix has a major effect on revenue flow.
- Use elective admission patterns to improve revenue forecasting and staffing schedules.


## 10. Conclusion

The dataset shows a stable and well-distributed patient profile, with strong revenue contribution from elective admissions, diabetes, and key Insurance Companies. The report provides a practical foundation for hospital management to monitor patient trends, improve resource allocation, and strengthen financial planning.
